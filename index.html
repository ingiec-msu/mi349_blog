Mapping Out My Future

Author: Ingie Charles

Published: 9/27/2025

As graduation approaches, I’ve started thinking seriously about where I want to spend my twenties — both for life and for my career. While I’m open to unexpected opportunities, I’ve narrowed down a few countries and cities that I’m most drawn to.

Seoul, Korea

Pros: I’m minoring in Korean and studied abroad here last fall. I love the food, public transport, and endless things to do.

Cons: It’s harder to find English-dominant jobs, and many foreigners don’t stay long-term, which can make lasting friendships difficult.

Austin, Texas

Pros: It’s close enough to visit family across states, and housing is affordable.

Cons: The heat is intense, there’s no reliable public transportation, and the bugs are huge.

London, United Kingdom

Pros: I’ve always wanted to visit Europe, and London’s architecture is stunning.

Cons: The lack of sunlight and cold weather would take getting used to.

Shanghai, China

Pros: I’ve been before and loved it — great public transport, beautiful city views, and easy access to nearby countries.

Cons: Internet censorship would be challenging.

Atlanta, Georgia

Pros: A comfortable place to settle down later in life, with amazing food options.

Cons: Housing costs are high.
