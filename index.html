Mapping Out My Future

Author: Ingie Charles

Published: 9/27/2025

As graduation approaches, I’ve started thinking seriously about where I want to spend my twenties — both for life and for my career. While I’m open to unexpected opportunities, I’ve narrowed down a few countries and cities that I’m most drawn to.

Seoul, Korea

Pros: I’m minoring in Korean and studied abroad here last fall. I love the food, public transport, and endless things to do.

Cons: It’s harder to find English-dominant jobs, and many foreigners don’t stay long-term, which can make lasting friendships difficult.

Austin, Texas

Pros: It’s close enough to visit family across states, and housing is affordable.

Cons: The heat is intense, there’s no reliable public transportation, and the bugs are huge.

London, United Kingdom

Pros: I’ve always wanted to visit Europe, and London’s architecture is stunning.

Cons: The lack of sunlight and cold weather would take getting used to.

Shanghai, China

Pros: I’ve been before and loved it — great public transport, beautiful city views, and easy access to nearby countries.

Cons: Internet censorship would be challenging.

Atlanta, Georgia

Pros: A comfortable place to settle down later in life, with amazing food options.

Cons: Housing costs are high.

A Gentle Reset: Building Habits That Last

Author: Ingie Charles

Published: 9/27/2025

Lately, I’ve been focusing on improving my health — both in how I eat and how I move. Since this is my final year at Michigan State University, I want to end it on a strong and healthy note by building habits that will carry into adulthood. I’ve failed at this goal before, but this time I’m setting clear, realistic rules to stay consistent: follow the 80/20 rule, train each body group twice a week, get seven hours of sleep, and practice self-forgiveness.

Follow the 80/20 rule

I still want to enjoy my favorite foods, so the 80/20 rule helps me stay balanced. Eighty percent of the time, I’ll focus on eating nutritious, protein-rich meals. The other 20 percent, I’ll enjoy the foods I love without guilt.

Train each body group twice a week

To reach the form I want, I plan to work both my upper and lower body twice a week using targeted exercises. Get at least seven hours of sleep

Proper rest is essential — muscles recover and grow during sleep, so I’m making it a priority.

Practice self-forgiveness

If I slip up or miss a workout, I won’t give up. Instead, I’ll forgive myself, learn from it, and try again the next day. Consistency, not perfection, is what matters most.

This time, I’m not chasing quick results because I’m building habits that last.
